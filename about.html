// Our story

Rooted in a love of plants and science.

The UW Plant Breeding Club was founded by graduate students who wanted a space to connect across departments, swap ideas, and celebrate the weird, wonderful world of plant genetics. What started as informal coffee chats has grown into a vibrant community of researchers, educators, and enthusiasts.

We believe that plant breeding is one of the most impactful disciplines in science — from food security to climate adaptation to the basic curiosity of how organisms work. Our club is a place to explore all of that together.

We're open to everyone: MS and PhD students, undergrads, postdocs, and faculty. If you find plants interesting, you belong here.

// What we're about

What we do

From field trips to speaker series, we keep things fun and relevant.

🔬

Seminars & Talks

Monthly presentations from grad students, faculty, and industry professionals on cutting-edge plant breeding topics.

🌾

Field Trips

Visits to seed companies, research farms, and UW facilities — getting us out of the lab and into the field.

🤝

Networking

Connecting members with internship and career opportunities in academia, industry, and the nonprofit sector.

📚

Journal Club

Casual discussions of recent papers — from new QTL studies to big-picture topics in breeding ethics and policy.

🌱

Community

Social events, potlucks, and hangouts that help you build friendships outside the lab bench.

📢

Outreach

Science communication and community education around plant science, food systems, and genetic technology.
